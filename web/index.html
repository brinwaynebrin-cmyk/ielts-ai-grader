The History of Tea

Passage 1 · 适合难度 · 建议用时 20 分钟

Tea, one of the world's most popular beverages, has a history that spans thousands of years. Legend has it that in 2737 BC, Chinese Emperor Shen Nung was sitting beneath a tree while his servant boiled drinking water. Some leaves from the tree blew into the water, and the emperor decided to try the infusion. The tree was Camellia sinensis, and the resulting drink was what we now call tea.

From its mythical origins, tea gradually spread throughout China and beyond. During the Tang Dynasty (618-907 AD), tea became the national drink of China. The first definitive book on tea, "The Classic of Tea" (Cha Jing), was written by Lu Yu in the 8th century. This work elevated tea from a mere beverage to a form of art and cultural practice.

Tea reached Japan in the 9th century when Buddhist monks brought tea seeds back from China. The Japanese tea ceremony, or chanoyu, developed as a spiritual and philosophical practice, emphasizing mindfulness and the beauty of imperfection.

European contact with tea began in the 16th century through Portuguese priests and merchants. However, it was the Dutch who first imported tea commercially to Europe in 1610. By the mid-17th century, tea had become fashionable among the British aristocracy, eventually becoming an integral part of British culture...
